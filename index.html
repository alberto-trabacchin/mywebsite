Something about me...

I obtained my bachelor's degree in Mechatronics Engineering from the University of Padova, in 2021. Currently, I am enrolled in a Master's program at the same university, continuing my studies in Mechatronics Engineering.

Since October 2022, I've been participating in a Double Degree Program organized by the T.I.M.E. Association which involves a collaboration between the University of Padova and the Yokohama National University. This program will terminate in 2024 with the attainment of both an Italian Master's degree in Mechatronics Engineering and a Japanese Master's degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science.

I am currently engaged in research at the Fujimoto Laboratory, working in partnership with Magna. Our focus is on enhancing Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS) using computer vision techniques. Specifically, our project aims to develop algorithms capable of identifying potentially hazardous situations on the road, enabling timely warnings for drivers.
